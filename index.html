General Secretary Nguyen Phu Trong

One of the most influential Vietnamese leaders

This tribute page honors the life and achievements of Nguyen Phu Trong, a prominent figure in Vietnamese politics who served as the General Secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam from 2011 to 2021. Trong's leadership was marked by his commitment to combating corruption and promoting economic development in Vietnam.

Infomation

Early life

1944

Born in Dong Anh, Ha Noi, Viet Nam

1963 - 1967

Studied and earned a Bachelor in Philology from Vietnam National University in Hanoi

1973 - 1976

Underwent a political-economic post-graduate course at the High-level Nguyễn Ái Quốc Party School

1981 - 1983

Studied and obtained a Candidate of Sciences degree from the Academy of Sciences in the Soviet Union

2000 - 2006

Served as the de facto chief of the city authority and was the secretary of the party's Executive Committee of Hanoi

June 26, 2006

Was elected as the Chairman of the National Assembly. During this period, he was elected secretary of the party organization in the National Assembly and member of the Council for Defence and Security

General Secretaryship

First term (2011–2016)

2011

Was elected General Secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam (CPV) at the 11th National Congress, becoming Vietnam's top leader

2012

Urged the CPV to adopt Resolution No. 12, emphasizing anti-corruption and self-criticism among party officials

Second term (2016–2021)

2016

On January 27, Trọng was re-elected as General Secretary for a second term, during which he launched a major anti-corruption campaign.

2017

Visited China in January, and Xi Jinping made a reciprocal visit to Vietnam in November

2019

Met two leaders as a neutral host state leader during the North Korea–United States Hanoi Summit

Third term (2021–2024)

2021

Re-elected for a third term as General Secretary on January 31, despite health concerns. The National Assembly relieved him of the presidency on April 2

2022

Vietnam maintained neutrality during the Russian invasion of Ukraine. Trọng visited China from October 30 to November 2, becoming the first foreign leader to meet Xi Jinping after the 20th CCP National Congress

2023

On January, Nguyễn Xuân Phúc resigned from the presidency amid corruption scandals. Trọng's ally Võ Văn Thưởng succeeded him in March. U.S. President Joe Biden visited Vietnam in September and met with Trọng
